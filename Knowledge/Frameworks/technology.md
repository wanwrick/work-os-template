## 1. Management Information Systems

### IS / IT / IM Framework
Three layers of technology in organizations:

| Layer | Scope | Key Questions |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **Information Systems (IS)** | Business processes + people + technology | How does work get done? |
| **Information Technology (IT)** | Hardware, software, networks, infrastructure | What tools do we use? |
| **Information Management (IM)** | Strategy, governance, policies, standards | How do we govern data and tech? |

**Key insight:** Technology alone doesn't create value. Value comes from how IS transforms business processes, enabled by IT and governed by IM.

### Digital Transformation Framework
```
Digital Transformation = Technology × Process Change × Culture Change
```

**Five domains of digital transformation:**
1. **Customer Experience:** Personalization, self-service, omnichannel
2. **Operations:** Automation, real-time data, predictive maintenance
3. **Business Model:** Platform, subscription, data-as-product
4. **Organization:** Agile teams, cross-functional, continuous learning
5. **Technology Infrastructure:** Cloud, APIs, microservices, data platform

### Agile Transformation
- **From Waterfall:** Sequential, plan-driven, change-resistant
- **To Agile:** Iterative, feedback-driven, change-embracing
- **Scaling Frameworks:** SAFe, LeSS, Spotify Model, Disciplined Agile
- **Key metrics:** Velocity, cycle time, deployment frequency, lead time to change

### Digital Leadership Competencies
1. **Digital Fluency:** Understand tech enough to lead strategic conversations
2. **Data-Driven Decision Making:** Use evidence over intuition
3. **Customer Centricity:** Start with customer experience, work backwards
4. **Innovation Mindset:** Fail fast, learn faster, iterate
5. **Ecosystem Thinking:** Build platforms and partnerships, not just products

### Case Memo Framework
Structure for technology case analysis:
1. **Situation:** What's happening? (facts, context)
2. **Problem/Opportunity:** What needs to be addressed?
3. **Alternatives:** At least 3 options with pros/cons
4. **Recommendation:** Your pick with clear justification
5. **Implementation:** How to execute (timeline, resources, risks)

## 2. AI Fluency

### AI/ML Fundamentals
```
AI (Artificial Intelligence)
  └── ML (Machine Learning)
        ├── Supervised Learning (labeled data → predictions)
        │     ├── Classification (categories)
        │     └── Regression (continuous values)
        ├── Unsupervised Learning (find patterns in unlabeled data)
        │     ├── Clustering
        │     └── Dimensionality Reduction
        └── Reinforcement Learning (learn by trial and reward)
              └── Foundation for game AI, robotics
```

### Large Language Models (LLMs)
- **Architecture:** Transformer-based neural networks trained on massive text corpora
- **Capabilities:** Text generation, summarization, translation, reasoning, code generation
- **Limitations:** Hallucination, knowledge cutoff, context window limits, lack of true understanding
- **Fine-tuning:** Adapt pre-trained model to specific domain/task
- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** Combine LLM with external knowledge retrieval

### Generative AI Strategy for Business
**Adoption Framework:**
1. **Identify use cases** by business function (marketing, ops, product, finance)
2. **Assess feasibility** (data availability, quality, model capability)
3. **Evaluate risk** (accuracy requirements, regulatory, ethical)
4. **Pilot and measure** (A/B test, control group, clear KPIs)
5. **Scale or pivot** based on results

**High-value enterprise AI use cases:**
- Document processing and extraction
- Customer service automation
- Code generation and review
- Data analysis and reporting
- Content creation and personalization
- Predictive analytics and forecasting

### AI Governance Framework
| Dimension | Key Questions | Controls |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| **Data** | Is training data appropriate and licensed? | Data lineage, consent tracking |
| **Model** | Is it accurate, fair, and reliable? | Testing, bias audits, monitoring |
| **Use** | Are applications appropriate and safe? | Usage policies, human oversight |
| **Compliance** | Does it meet regulatory requirements? | Audit trails, risk assessments |
| **Ethics** | Is it aligned with organizational values? | Ethics review board, guidelines |

### Prompt Engineering Principles
1. **Be specific:** Clear instructions produce better results
2. **Provide context:** Background information improves accuracy
3. **Use examples:** Show desired format/output (few-shot)
4. **Chain of thought:** Ask for step-by-step reasoning
5. **Iterate:** Refine prompts based on outputs
6. **Set constraints:** Define scope, format, length, tone

## 3. Sustainability & Technology

### ESG Integration in Technology Decisions
- **Environmental:** Cloud efficiency, energy consumption, carbon footprint of compute
- **Social:** Digital inclusion, data privacy, algorithmic fairness, labor impact of automation
- **Governance:** Data governance, AI ethics, cybersecurity, regulatory compliance

### Decarbonization Through Technology
- **Cloud migration:** Hyperscalers achieve better energy efficiency than on-premise
- **Data optimization:** Reduce unnecessary data storage and processing
- **Green software:** Efficient code reduces compute resources needed
- **Measurement:** Track and report technology carbon footprint

### Circular Economy in Tech
- Hardware lifecycle management
- Cloud resource optimization (right-sizing, auto-scaling)
- Data lifecycle management (retention policies, archive strategies)
- Reduce, reuse, recycle applies to data and infrastructure

### Triple Bottom Line
```
People → Planet → Profit
```
Technology decisions should consider impact on all three, not just financial returns.
