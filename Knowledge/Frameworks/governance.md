# Corporate Governance -- Knowledge Base

> Covers agency theory, board composition, executive compensation, shareholder activism, and anti-takeover defenses.

---

## 1. Foundations

### Agency Theory
**The Problem:** Separation of ownership (shareholders) and control (managers) creates conflicts of interest.

**Agent:** Manager acts on behalf of **Principal:** Shareholders

**Agency Costs:**
1. **Monitoring costs:** Board oversight, audits, reporting requirements
2. **Bonding costs:** Incentive alignment mechanisms (stock options, clawbacks)
3. **Residual loss:** Value destroyed despite monitoring and bonding

### Corporate Objective Function (Jensen)
- **Shareholder Value Maximization:** Firm should maximize long-run value of equity
- **Stakeholder Theory:** Consider all stakeholders (employees, customers, community, environment)
- **Enlightened Shareholder Value:** Maximize shareholder value by serving stakeholders well (long-term view)
- **Debate:** Short-term profit vs. long-term value creation; which stakeholders to prioritize

### Governance 2.0 (Subramanian)
Modern governance goes beyond traditional board oversight:
- **Shareholder engagement:** Active dialogue, not just proxy voting
- **Board effectiveness:** Composition, diversity, independence, expertise
- **Executive accountability:** Pay-for-performance alignment
- **Stakeholder integration:** ESG factors in governance decisions
- **Transparency:** Disclosure beyond regulatory minimum

## 2. Board of Directors

### Board Composition
| Characteristic | Best Practice | Rationale |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Independence** | Majority independent | Reduce management influence |
| **Size** | 7-12 members | Balance diversity with efficiency |
| **Diversity** | Gender, racial, expertise mix | Better decision-making, representation |
| **Expertise** | Industry, finance, technology, legal | Informed oversight |
| **Tenure** | Mix of new and seasoned | Fresh perspective + institutional knowledge |
| **Separation** | Independent chair or lead director | Check on CEO power |

### Board Committees
- **Audit Committee:** Financial reporting integrity, internal controls, external audit
- **Compensation Committee:** Executive pay design, performance metrics, peer benchmarking
- **Nominating/Governance Committee:** Board composition, director selection, governance policies
- **Risk Committee:** Enterprise risk oversight (increasingly required in financial services)

### Board Effectiveness
- **Oversight, not management:** Board advises and monitors; management executes
- **Information asymmetry:** Challenge: managers know more than directors
- **Red flags:** Rubber-stamp board, CEO dominance, lack of executive sessions, inadequate preparation
- **Best practices:** Annual board evaluation, director education, strategy deep-dives, site visits

## 3. Executive Compensation

### Compensation Components
| Component | Purpose | Risk to Executive |
|-----------|---------|------------------|
| **Base Salary** | Fixed income, market competitive | None |
| **Annual Bonus** | Short-term performance incentive | Moderate (tied to annual metrics) |
| **Stock Options** | Long-term alignment with shareholders | High (value depends on stock price) |
| **Restricted Stock** | Retention + alignment | Moderate (forfeited if leave early) |
| **Performance Shares** | Long-term value creation | High (tied to multi-year targets) |
| **Benefits/Perks** | Competitive positioning | None |

### Pay-for-Performance Alignment
```
Good Design: Incentive metrics → Behavior change → Value creation → Shareholder returns
Bad Design: Incentive gaming → Short-term thinking → Value destruction → Misalignment
```

**Key metrics for incentive plans:**
- TSR (Total Shareholder Return), relative and absolute
- EPS growth
- ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)
- Revenue growth
- EVA (Economic Value Added)
- Strategic milestones (non-financial)

### Say-on-Pay
- Shareholder advisory vote on executive compensation (non-binding but influential)
- Failed say-on-pay → reputational damage, board engagement required
- Proxy advisory firms (ISS, Glass Lewis) influence outcomes significantly

### Groysberg Compensation Framework
- **Market benchmarking:** Compare to peer group (50th-75th percentile typical)
- **Internal equity:** Fair relative to other executives in the firm
- **Pay mix:** Balance of fixed vs. variable, short-term vs. long-term
- **Performance linkage:** Clear connection between metrics and payout
- **Retention value:** Golden handcuffs vs. excessive guarantees

## 4. Shareholder Activism

### Types of Activists
| Type | Approach | Goal |
|------|----------|------|
| **Hedge Fund Activists** | Take position, push for change | Unlock shareholder value (financial) |
| **Institutional Investors** | Engagement, proxy voting | Long-term governance improvement |
| **ESG/Social Activists** | Shareholder proposals, campaigns | Environmental/social change |
| **Proxy Contests** | Nominate alternative directors | Change board composition/strategy |

### Activist Playbook
1. Build position quietly (below 5% disclosure threshold)
2. File 13D (disclose activist intent)
3. Send public letter outlining demands
4. Nominate alternative board slate
5. Campaign for proxy votes
6. Settlement or proxy fight

### Common Activist Demands
- Board seats for activist nominees
- Strategic review or sale of company
- Operational improvements (cost cutting)
- Capital allocation changes (buybacks, dividends, divestitures)
- CEO/management changes
- Governance reforms

## 5. Anti-Takeover Defenses

### Defense Mechanisms
| Defense | How It Works | Shareholder View |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Poison Pill** | Dilutes hostile acquirer's stake | Controversial: entrenches management? |
| **Staggered Board** | Only 1/3 of directors up for election annually | Delays hostile takeover by years |
| **Dual-Class Stock** | Founders retain voting control with minority ownership | Limits shareholder democracy |
| **Golden Parachute** | Large payout if executive terminated after acquisition | Can discourage takeovers |
| **White Knight** | Invite friendly acquirer to outbid hostile bidder | Better deal for shareholders? |
| **Pac-Man Defense** | Target makes counter-bid for acquirer | Rarely used, dramatic |
| **Crown Jewels** | Sell key assets to make target less attractive | Destroys value |

### Shareholder Rights & Protections
- **Proxy Access:** Shareholders can nominate directors on company's proxy card
- **Majority Voting:** Directors need >50% support (vs. plurality)
- **Board Declassification:** Annual election of all directors
- **Separation of Chair/CEO:** Independent oversight
- **Special Meeting Rights:** Shareholders can call meetings

## 6. Value Maximization Framework

### TSR Decomposition
```
TSR = Revenue Growth + Margin Expansion + Multiple Expansion + Dividends/Buybacks
```

### EVA as Governance Metric
```
EVA = NOPAT - (Capital × WACC)
```
Positive EVA = Creating value above cost of capital. Use for business unit evaluation and capital allocation.

### Capital Allocation Framework
Priority order for free cash flow:
1. Invest in positive NPV projects (organic growth)
2. Pursue strategic M&A (if synergies > premium)
3. Return capital to shareholders (buybacks if undervalued, dividends if stable)
4. Pay down debt (if overleveraged)
5. Build cash reserves (if uncertain environment)

## Case Studies Referenced

### Tesla Executive Compensation (2018-2024)
- Elon Musk's 2018 compensation plan: All performance-based, no salary
- 12 tranches tied to market cap and revenue/EBITDA milestones
- Shareholder-approved but controversial (largest CEO pay package ever)
- 2024 Delaware court voided it; Texas re-vote approved it

### Shake Shack Proxy Battle (2022)
- Activist pushed for operational improvements and governance changes
- Illustrates tension between founder vision and shareholder value maximization

### SVB (Silicon Valley Bank) Failure (2023)
- Board governance failures: risk committee expertise, risk appetite framework
- Concentration risk (tech/startup deposits), interest rate risk mismanagement
- Lessons for board oversight of enterprise risk
