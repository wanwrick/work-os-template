## 1. Marketing Management

### STP Framework (Segmentation → Targeting → Positioning)

**Segmentation Bases:**
- **Geographic:** Region, city size, climate, density
- **Demographic:** Age, income, education, family size, occupation
- **Psychographic:** Lifestyle, personality, values, attitudes
- **Behavioral:** Usage rate, loyalty, benefits sought, occasion

**Targeting Strategies:**
- **Undifferentiated:** One offer for entire market (mass marketing)
- **Differentiated:** Different offers for different segments
- **Concentrated:** One segment, deep focus (niche)
- **Micromarketing:** Individualized offerings (1-to-1)

**Positioning Statement Template:**
```
For [target segment] who [need/want],
[Brand] is the [category] that [key benefit]
because [reason to believe].
```

### Marketing Mix (4Ps)
| P | Strategic Questions |
|---|-------------------|
| **Product** | What are we selling? Features, quality, design, packaging, service |
| **Price** | What do customers pay? Pricing strategy, discounts, payment terms |
| **Place** | How do customers get it? Channels, distribution, coverage, locations |
| **Promotion** | How do they learn about it? Advertising, PR, sales, digital |

### Pricing Strategies
- **Cost-Plus:** Cost + markup (simple but ignores demand)
- **Value-Based:** Price based on customer's perceived value
- **Competitive:** Match or undercut competitors
- **Penetration:** Low price to gain share, raise later
- **Skimming:** High price initially, lower over time
- **Dynamic:** Real-time adjustment based on demand
- **Freemium:** Free base product, premium features paid

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) & Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
```
CLV  = (ARPC × GM) / Churn Rate
CAC  = Total Sales & Marketing Cost / New Customers Acquired
Payback = CAC / (Monthly Revenue per Customer × GM)
```
Where: ARPC = Average Revenue Per Customer ($/yr), GM = Gross Margin (decimal), Churn Rate = annual customer loss rate (decimal).

**Benchmark:** LTV:CAC ratio > 3:1 indicates healthy unit economics. Below 1:1 means you lose money on every customer acquired.

### Brand Equity (Keller's Model)
```
Brand Salience (Identity) → Who are you?
    ↓
Brand Performance + Brand Imagery (Meaning) → What are you?
    ↓
Brand Judgments + Brand Feelings (Response) → What about you?
    ↓
Brand Resonance (Relationships) → What about you and me?
```

### Consumer Behavior
- **Problem Recognition → Information Search → Evaluation → Purchase → Post-Purchase**
- **Bounded Rationality:** People use heuristics, not optimization
- **Loss Aversion:** Losses hurt ~2x more than equivalent gains feel good
- **Anchoring:** First number seen influences subsequent judgments
- **Social Proof:** People follow what others do
- **Framing Effect:** How options are presented changes choices

## 2. Marketing Strategy

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
People don't buy products; they hire them to do a job.

**Framework:**
```
When [situation], I want to [motivation/job], so I can [expected outcome].
```

**Three dimensions of every job:**
- **Functional:** The practical task to accomplish
- **Emotional:** How the customer wants to feel
- **Social:** How the customer wants to be perceived

**Application:**
1. Identify the job customers are hiring your product for
2. Find the struggling moments (when current solutions fail)
3. Design around the job, not the product features
4. Compete against all alternatives (not just direct competitors)

### Consumer Decision Journey (McKinsey)
Non-linear path replacing the traditional funnel:
```
Initial Consideration → Active Evaluation → Moment of Purchase → Post-Purchase Experience
       ↑                                                                    │
       └────────────── Loyalty Loop (repurchase without re-evaluation) ────┘
```

**Touchpoint Strategy:**
- Identify all touchpoints along the journey
- Prioritize based on influence and cost
- Design for consistency across touchpoints
- Measure conversion at each stage

### Design Thinking for Marketing
```
Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
```
- **Empathize:** Deep customer research (ethnography, interviews, observation)
- **Define:** Articulate the real problem from customer's perspective
- **Ideate:** Generate many solutions without judgment
- **Prototype:** Build quick, cheap, testable versions
- **Test:** Learn from real customer interaction

### A/B Testing & Experimentation
```
1. Formulate hypothesis: "Changing X will increase Y by Z%"
2. Define success metric (primary + guardrails)
3. Calculate sample size for statistical significance
4. Run test (randomize, control, measure)
5. Analyze results (p-value < 0.05, confidence interval)
6. Implement or iterate
```

### MarTech & Generative AI in Marketing
- **Content personalization at scale**: AI-generated variations for segments
- **Predictive analytics**: Churn prediction, next-best-action, lead scoring
- **Customer insights**: Sentiment analysis, topic modeling, journey analytics
- **Creative generation**: Ad copy, email subject lines, social content
- **Attribution modeling**: Multi-touch attribution across channels

### Outside-In Strategy
Start with the customer, work backwards to capabilities:
```
Customer Needs → Value Proposition → Required Capabilities → Strategy
(Not: Our Capabilities → What Can We Sell?)
```

## Quick Reference: When to Use What

| Situation | Framework | Section |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| "Who should we target?" | STP, segmentation bases | Marketing Management |
| "How should we price this?" | Value-based, competitive, CLV/CAC | Marketing Management |
| "What do customers really want?" | JTBD, empathy mapping | Marketing Strategy |
| "How do customers decide?" | Consumer Decision Journey, touchpoints | Marketing Strategy |
| "How do we position our brand?" | Positioning statement, brand equity | Marketing Management |
| "Should we run this experiment?" | A/B testing framework, hypothesis | Marketing Strategy |
| "How do we innovate our offering?" | Design thinking, JTBD | Marketing Strategy |
| "Is our marketing working?" | CLV/CAC, attribution, funnel metrics | Both |
